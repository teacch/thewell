Our Mission

The Nigerian educational system is grappling with a severe and ongoing crisis, marked by widespread learning losses and learning poverty among millions of children. This dire situation results from a combination of factors, all of which can be effectively addressed through tested and proven interventions

The inextricable link between education and poverty is unmistakable, as education plays a pivotal role in national development. The Covid-19 pandemic has exacerbated education inequality on a global scale, intensifying poverty levels, increasing crime rates, and contributing to a rising number of Nigerian children experiencing learning loss and learning poverty. Urgent and targeted interventions are required to address this crisis, ensuring that Nigeria's education system becomes a catalyst for progress and equity, rather than a source of perpetuating inequality and poverty

Problem Statement

The educational system in Nigeria is facing a severe and persistent crisis characterized by alarming rates of learning losses and learning poverty among millions of children. This crisis is primarily attributed to a multitude of factors, including a significant out-of-school population, which was estimated at 13 million children before the Covid-19 pandemic and surged to 36 million when schools were forced to close due to the pandemic.

Nigeria grapples with a critical shortage of 277,537 teachers within its basic education sector, underscoring a substantial gap in the availability of educators. Teachers are widely recognized as instrumental agents for ensuring equity, accessibility, and quality in education, making them pivotal for sustainable global development.
